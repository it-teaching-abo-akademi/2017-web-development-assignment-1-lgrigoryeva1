Seoul (/soʊl/; 서울; Korean: [s⁽ʰ⁾ʌul] (About this sound listen)), officially the Seoul Special Metropolitan City – is the capital and largest metropolis of the Republic of Korea (commonly known as South Korea).[1] Seoul is the world's 16th largest city,[10] and forms the heart of the Seoul Capital Area, which includes the surrounding Incheon metropolis and Gyeonggi province. The Seoul Capital Area houses about half of the country's population of 51.44 million people as well as having 678,102 international residents.[11]
